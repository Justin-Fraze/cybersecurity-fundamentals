Common Cyber Threats

Cyber threats are attempts to damage, steal, or disrupt digital systems.

Phishing Attacks

Phishing is a form of social engineering where attackers deceive users into revealing sensitive information, such as login credentials or credit card numbers. These attacks typically arrive via fraudulent emails, text messages (smishing), or phone calls (vishing) that appear to be from a trusted source like a bank or a coworker.

How to stay safe: Always verify the sender's email address, avoid clicking suspicious links, and never provide passwords over email.

Malware

Short for "malicious software," malware is a broad term for any code designed to infiltrate, damage, or gain unauthorized access to a computer system. Common types include viruses, worms, and Trojans. Malware can track your keystrokes, steal your files, or turn your computer into a "bot" for larger cyberattacks.

How to stay safe: Keep your operating system updated and use a reputable antivirus program to scan all downloaded files.

Ransomware

Ransomware is a specific, high-stakes type of malware that encrypts a victim’s files, making them inaccessible. The attacker then demands a "ransom" payment (usually in cryptocurrency) in exchange for the decryption key. There is no guarantee that paying the ransom will actually return your data.

How to stay safe: The best defense is a regular, "offline" backup of your most important data. If you have a backup, the attacker loses their leverage.

Password Attacks

These attacks involve various methods to crack or bypass your login security. This includes Brute Force (trying every possible combination), Dictionary Attacks (using lists of common words), and Credential Stuffing (using passwords leaked from other site breaches).

How to stay safe: Use a unique, complex password for every account. The most effective defense is enabling Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), which stops an attacker even if they have your password.
